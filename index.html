What Happy People Know

Fear is the root of unhappiness which is in a reptilian part of the brain leading primal instincts to dictate negative reactions. However, just because humans are “hardwired for hard times” doesn’t mean being trapped in unhappiness. The author, Dan Baker, wants people to know they can break the cycles of stress and teach themselves to to be happy. People can participate in their own destiny if managing their life is set as a primary goal.

Instead of wasting energy on struggles it’s important to learn traps to avoid such as attempts to buy happiness. Instead there are six powerful happiness tools:appreciation, choice, personal power, leading with strengths, language and stories, and multidimensional living.

The author utilizes case studies to demonstrate his strategy and the results are powerful. If you want to devise a plan to overcome unhappiness this book will teach you to identify the happiness traps which is the first step. Then you’ll learn six simple happiness tools along with twelve qualities for happiness. Once these tools are put into place they’ll lead to courage, humor and optimism.

Getting Things Done

With over 1.5 million copies sold, David Allen’s book is known to many as the bible of productivity. David Allen has been an executive coach and management consultant for over 20 years, and is widely known as the productivity guru. It was originally published in 2001 and has seen been revised to reflect modern changes in technology. The Getting Things Done (GTD) system is timeless and can be implemented instantly using a smartphone, paper or online.

GTD has three parts with the first providing an outline to get control of your life with the five stages of mastering workflow: collection, processing, organizing, reviewing and doing. The second part spans the majority of the book and provides in-depth detail on the methodology and application. The final section explains the benefits gained from using this approach and why they’ve been tested and proven to work.

This easy to follow system includes an illustrated flowchart that summarizes the process inputs, outputs and actions to be taken. If anyone thinks they don’t have time to learn a system to optimize their productivity this book includes the optimum two-minute rule everyone should utilize!

The Shift

Wayne Dyer started his career as a high school guidance counselor and a professor of counseling psychology, He was an American philosopher, self-help author and motivational speaker. The Shift, is a companion book to the movie of the same name focused on helping people make a shift towards their highest potential. The book illustrates how to move from a state of ambition to meaning and why its important.

A shift of this nature involves moving from ego-directed to life being influenced primarily by purpose. This kind of shift highlights spiritual connectedness and eliminates feels of separateness. Falls and low points in life provide needed energy to move away from ego and towards a life of purpose and meaning.

The ego’s agenda is full of false promises and never-ending demands and The Shift provides the transition to make a commitment to living life based on feeling purpose and experiencing meaning, the moments of our existence.

Harry Potter and the Sourcers Stone

J.K. Rowling is a British author and creator of the critically acclaimed Harry Potter series. Harry Potter was orphaned as an infant and shunned by his relatives. He’s a lonely and miserable boy forced to wear hand-me-down clothes from his cousin Dudley and live in a cupboard under the stairs. However his world changes on his eleventh birthday when he’s informed he’s a wizard and will soon be attending Hogwarts School of Witchcraft and Wizardry.

Harry doesn’t know what to believe when he’s informed he’s a hero in the wizarding world. As an infant, his parents were killed by Lord Voldemort who also tried to kill Harry but failed. Everyone is amazed to learn Harry survived and allegedly destroyed Voldemort in the process. Once Harry arrives at wizarding school he learns everything he’s been told is true. It doesn’t stop him from having a joyful heart and he quickly becomes best friends with Rob Weasly and Hermione Granger.

The trio break school rules and do their best to stay on top of the mounds of homework while solving a mystery of the gigantic three-headed dog guarding a mysterious hidden door. Hermione and Ron help Harry advance through the various challenges to stop a thief but the ultimate battle is something Harry has to do alone.

Memoirs of a Geisha

This historical novel written by American author Arthur Golden tells the story in first person of a fictional geisha working in Kyoto, Japan. The story takes place before and after World War II and readers enter the world of a young girl, Chiyo, whose mother passes resulting in her being separated from her sister and sold into slavery to a geisha house in the Gion district. She spends her time as a servant and then begins training as a geisha.

Readers will witness Chiyo’s transformation as she learns the arts of the geisha in a culture considered synonymous with prostitution by outsiders. The geisha is primarily an entertainer who is trained in the arts of tea ceremony, dance and singing. In a world with theaters and marvelous teahouses, ornate temples, artists’ streets and narrow back alleys geisha’s can be found with their elaborate makeup and hair, wearing a kimono and pouring sake to reveal just a touch of inner wrist. Appearances are paramount as women are trained to beguile the most powerful men, girl’s virginity is auctioned to the highest bidder and love is scorned as an illusion.

After World Ware II geisha houses are forced to close and Chiyo must reinvent herself all over again. She learns that her own feelings do matter and honoring them results in an intelligent, well-earned happy ending. This is a unique work of fiction thats triumphant. It’s suspenseful and romantic yet completely unforgettable.
